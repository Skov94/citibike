Introduction

You've probably seen them out there in the big city picture - the citybikes. You've probably also wondered who these people are that would rather lend a bike, than buy one, and what they use them for? Well, we are just about to find out..

We will be exploring Citi bike trip data for trips started in Jersey City in 2017 January-October (235.996 trips), which can be downloaded from the Citi bike NYC webpage. This dataset includes:

  • Start time and date
  • Start station name
  • End station name
  • Station Lat/Long
  • Gender (1:male, 2:female)
  • Year of birth

With this dataset, we will be able to answer tons of questions, where the visualizations below e.g. could help us answer such questions as "Which age group bikes the most?", "Which routes are most popular at certain times of the day?", "Where do young females bike on sundays?", "Which day of the week are most rides taken on?" or "Which stations are most popular?" - which all is going to help us explore who uses the bikes and what they use them for.

Where do people bike to?

The second visualization shows the flow of the trips, on the 100 most popular routes, based on the selections above. The stations are sized relative to the number of bike pickups and dropoffs, and colored based on whether they mainly are a dropoff or pickoff station. This means that the direction of the routes is indicated by moving from the more green towards the more red dots. The 25 most popular routes are visualized as lines.

If you haven't noticed it already Jersey City has a very classic commuter flow pattern, where commuters bike towards bigger cities in the morning (with better transportation options), and the opposite at the end of the day. This pattern can easily be seen by comparing the flow seen when selecting the early hour peak on the third graph (and the weekdays on the second graph), with the one seen when selecting the late hour peak.
